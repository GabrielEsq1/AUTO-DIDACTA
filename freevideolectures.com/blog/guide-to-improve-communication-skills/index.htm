Guide to improve communication skills

By admin April 27, 2015 Guide, languages 2 Comments

What did Mahatma Gandhi, Martin Luther king Jr and Swami Vivekananda have in common? Apart from being great leaders of their times, they were also exceptional orators. They could keep the audiences captivated and spell bound with their inspiring speeches. That brings us to the topic of what are the prerequisites to have good communication skills?

Communication skills refer to the ability to convey information to another effectively and efficiently. The benefits of good communication are too numerous to list, for they enhance all aspects of life from the personal to the professional.

In today’s day and age, there is no denying that it is extremely important to be skilled in the art of efficient communication as it is vital to the success of any endeavor.

In our day to day life the various forms communication that we involve in, most often are spoken, written, public speaking, Business communication and the likes. The basic premise of all these forms ultimately remains the same. That is to make yourself clearly understood.

5 basic steps to Good Communication skills.

Listen: This is an important tool because learning how to value different viewpoints and maintaining an open mind can help us become better communicators.

Connect: Become genuinely interested in other people and show appreciation. A real sense of connection can really a difference in the outcome of the communications.

 Practice: A time tested method and absolutely no substitute to it! Be it in front of the mirror, communicating with peers and acquaintances or taking the lead in a group discussion, these are the best possible practical ways to improve yourself.  Also use technology constructively to communicate you opinions and viewpoints in the form of blogging, micro-blogging etc.

Request Feedback: It is always good to confirm that there is a mutual understanding of what’s being communicated as there are often times when we are completely misunderstood or the vice-versa. Always try to end your communication in a friendly manner to leave the other individual feeling understood and valued.

Stay positive: This is important for any kind of productive communication. It is easy get bogged down by failure, or when people shut you down. Be constructive and have belief as there is always scope for improvement in any situation.

Non-verbal communication

Sometimes our body language, facial expressions, gestures and even posture can seriously undermine the message contained in our words if we not careful to control them.

A weak hand shake could mean lack of confidence or frowned eyebrows can show displeasure! So it is important to learn how to use non-verbal methods to communicate more effectively.
